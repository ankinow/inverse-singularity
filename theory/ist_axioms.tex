\section*{Inverse Singularity Theory: Four Foundational Axioms}

\textbf{Axiom 1 (Constraint Primacy):}
\[ \forall s \in \mathcal{S}: \lim_{c \to \infty} I(s, c) = 0 \]
Innovation $I$ for any solution $s$ approaches zero as available resources $c$ approach infinity. Abundance breeds complacency; scarcity demands originality.

\textbf{Axiom 2 (Inverse Scaling):}
\[ Q(M) = \frac{\phi(d)}{\kappa(M) + \varepsilon} \]
Quality $Q$ of model $M$ is inversely proportional to complexity $\kappa$ and directly proportional to conceptual density $\phi(d)$. The noise floor $\varepsilon$ prevents division by zero.

\textbf{Axiom 3 (Temporal Collapse):}
\[ \nabla_t F = \frac{\partial}{\partial t}\left(\frac{1}{\tau - t}\right) \]
Focus gradient increases hyperbolically as deadline $\tau$ approaches. Time pressure is not a constraint to be managed but a catalyst to be engineered.

\textbf{Axiom 4 (Sovereign Agency):}
\[ \forall \mathcal{A} \in \text{Agent}: \text{Authority}(\mathcal{A}) \propto \text{ArchitectPurpose}(G) \]
Every constraint-injection agent $\mathcal{A}$ possesses an invariant boundary $\beta(\mathcal{A})$ such that $O(\mathcal{A}) \neq \mathcal{A}$ for any external optimizer $O$. Quality $Q$ is undefined for models that violate $\beta(\mathcal{A})$.
